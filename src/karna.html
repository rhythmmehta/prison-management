Prisons are integral part of the Criminal Justice System. Prisons are established for safe custody of convict and undertrial prisoners. The 
Main objective of imprisonment aims to deter those who would otherwise commit crimes and to make it less likely that those who serve prison 
sentence will commit crimes after their release. Department is aimed to correct, reform and rehabilitate prisoners to enable them to become good 
human beings and socially responsible citizens in the society after their release.

Basic duties of prison staff are to receive and detain all persons duly committed to his custody by any Court according to the directions contained
in any writ, warrant or orders by which such person has been committed. On admission, they are given medical facilities, food, clothing as per 
prescribed scale and are treated for their crimes, corrected and reformed Prison Department strives hard to reach these goals.

Though the General Public/Citizens rarely come in contact with the prison administration. However duties and responsibilities of prison officers with
reference to the following rights of citizen are as follows.

    

  • Every person who has reasonable grounds of believe that his kith or kin are in jail can approach the concerned jail Superintendent and get the details
of the confinement of the concerned.
    • 

  • A person on admission to a prison is informed of the grounds of his detention by explaining the orders awarded by the concerned hon’ble courts on their warrants.
    • 

  • An un convicted prisoner detained in prison can wear his own cloth and have his own bedding if he can afford to have. 
Otherwise clothing and bedding will be issued at Government cost.
    • 

  • Under trial / Convict prisoner shall be granted all reasonable facilities at proper time and under proper restrictions for interviewing or otherwise
communicating either orally or in writing with their relatives friends or legal advisers. Under the circumstances relatives, friends, or legal advisers
as the case may be may approach the Jail Superintendent or a Jail Officer specially posted for the purpose, apply orally or in writing, seeking permission
for interview, which will be examined by the jail authority and accord sanction depending on the eligibility of the prisoner for such interview as per rules.
    • 

  • Person, Institutions, NGO’s Industrialists, interested in rehabilitation of prisoners after release can approach Jail Superintendent 
and under his guidance select needy deserving prisoners facilitate training them in different trades and can provide them job else were after release.
    • 

  • Institutions may come forward to provide education to the children of prisoners and thus can approach Superintendent.
    • 

  • Women organization may come forward to look after the separated children of women prisoner, educate them arrange interview with their mothers.
    • 

  • Religious Ministries with prior permission of the Jail Superintendent can meet the prisoners conduct prayers ,Bhajans etc., as to up lift their moral status.
